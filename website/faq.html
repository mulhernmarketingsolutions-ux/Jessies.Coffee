Frequently asked questions

Licensing, booking, and the little logistics — if you don't see your question here, just ask us directly.

Licensing & cottage food

Are the syrups made in a licensed commercial kitchen?

No — our syrups are made in a home kitchen under the Colorado Cottage Foods Act, which allows small producers to make and sell certain low-risk foods directly to consumers without a commercial kitchen license. That means the kitchen is not inspected by the State of Colorado, which is standard for cottage food producers and is noted on every bottle.

What does the Colorado Cottage Foods Act actually allow?

It allows home producers to sell directly to end consumers — at farmers markets, online with local pickup or delivery, or in person — up to an annual gross sales limit set by the state. It doesn't cover wholesale sales to retail stores or restaurants, and it doesn't apply to the coffee & matcha bar service itself, which operates under a separate mobile food license (see below).

Is the mobile coffee & matcha cart licensed and insured?

Yes. The cart operates as a fully licensed mobile food unit and carries general liability insurance. A certificate of insurance is available on request for venues that require one for weddings or corporate bookings.

How should I store the syrups, and how long do they last?

Check the label on your bottle for specific storage and best-by guidance, since it can vary by flavor and batch. As a general rule, refrigerate after opening and use within a few weeks for the best flavor.

Booking an event

How far in advance should I book?

For weddings and larger corporate events, 4–6+ weeks out is ideal, especially during peak wedding season. Smaller or more flexible events can often be booked with less notice — reach out and we'll let you know what's available.

Do you require a deposit?

A deposit may be required to hold your date, along with a few booking terms specific to your event. All of that is spelled out clearly in your written quote before anything is confirmed — nothing is charged automatically from an inquiry form.

What's your cancellation or rescheduling policy?

This is handled on a per-event basis and included in your written quote. If your plans change, reach out as early as possible and we'll work with you.

What do you need from my venue?

Typically, standard outlet access and roughly a 10x10 ft. setup space. Water access is a nice-to-have but not required. If your venue has unusual constraints, just mention them when you request a quote and we'll plan around them.

What happens if it rains, or my event is outdoors?

The cart can run outdoors with adequate cover (a tent or overhang) to protect the equipment. We'll talk through a weather plan with you as part of booking any outdoor event.

Menu & dietary needs

Can I just buy bottled syrups without booking the full bar?

Yes — bottled syrups are sold on their own at farmers markets around South Denver, no event booking required. See the menu page for current flavors and where to find us.

Do you offer dairy-free or alternative milk options?

Yes — alternative milks are available. Let us know your preferences when we build your custom event menu, or just ask at the cart.

Can you build a fully custom drink menu for my event?

Absolutely — that's the default, actually. We build a menu around your date, guest count, and any signature flavor requests, using our house syrups or your own ideas.

Pricing

How much does it cost to book Jessie's for an event?

Pricing is simple and per-person, based on how long you need service. See the pricing page for a full breakdown by tier, or request a quote for exact numbers.

Does the per-person price change with guest count?

Yes — smaller groups run toward the higher end per person since setup and travel costs are spread over fewer guests, while larger groups trend lower. Every quote is finalized around your actual guest count.
